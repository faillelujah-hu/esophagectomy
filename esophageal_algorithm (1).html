Esophageal & Gastric Cancer Surgery
Perioperative Clinical Algorithm — Single Centre
Phase A
Preoperative Assessment & Optimisation
Early preoperative period — complete before final operative scheduling
Steps 1–2: Confirm pathway & baseline profile
Step 1
Confirm Curative Surgical Pathway
  • Histological diagnosis confirmed
  • Full staging completed (CT, PET-CT, EUS, laparoscopy if indicated)
  • MDT decision documented with date
  • Treatment sequence defined (upfront vs post-neoadjuvant)
  • Intended operation and curative intent recorded
⛔ Do not schedule surgery until MDT decision and operative intent are fully documented
🎯 Centre target: 100% MDT review before final operative scheduling
Step 2
Collect Mandatory Baseline Profile
DEMOGRAPHICS
  • Age, sex, height, weight, BMI
  • ECOG / functional status
  • ASA grade
  • Smoking & alcohol history
  • Prior thoracic/abdominal surgery
TUMOUR
  • Site, histology, clinical stage
  • Siewert type (GEJ tumours)
  • Neoadjuvant treatment history
  • Planned procedure & approach
BASELINE LABS
  • CBC, creatinine/eGFR, electrolytes, albumin
  • Iron studies / B12 / folate if anaemia present or suspected
  • LFTs if indicated; coagulation if relevant
Steps 3.1–3.5: Structured risk stratification (five domains)
⬡ 3.1 General / Anaesthetic Risk ▶
  • ASA grade, ECOG status
  • Coronary disease, heart failure, arrhythmia
  • Diabetes, COPD/asthma, CKD
  • Anticoagulants/antiplatelets, steroids
  • Prior thoracic surgery
  • Frailty signal — use formal tool (CFS, mFI)
⚠ ASA III–IV = mandatory consultant anaesthetic review. ECOG ≥2 = formal fitness discussion required
⛔ Unstable cardiac, respiratory, or renal disease = not ready for final scheduling
💡 Frailty, not chronological age alone, should drive risk stratification in older patients
⬡ 3.2 Nutritional Risk ▶
  • Unintentional weight loss (amount & duration)
  • Current oral intake, dysphagia severity
  • Regurgitation, vomiting, GOO symptoms
  • Albumin, sarcopenia risk
  • Current nutritional support if any
⛔ Red flags: oral intake <50% for >7 days; expected nil orally ≥5 days perioperatively; severe malnutrition; major GOO
🎯 100% dietitian review for every at-risk patient. Formal nutrition plan before final operation date confirmed.
⬡ 3.3 Sarcopenia & Frailty ▶
  • Formal frailty tool (one unit-wide tool)
  • CT body composition where feasible (gold standard)
  • Handgrip strength / chair-rise test
  • Mobility and daily function
⚠ High-risk thresholds: 6MWT <400 m; anaerobic threshold ≤11.1 mL/kg/min on CPET
🎯 All high-risk patients enter a prehabilitation pathway. CT body composition reviewed when imaging + software available.
⬡ 3.4 Cardiorespiratory Reserve ▶
  • Baseline respiratory symptoms & exercise tolerance
  • Oxygen saturation, COPD/asthma history
  • PFTs if indicated (obstructive/restrictive pattern)
  • Echocardiogram if indicated (LV function, valve disease)
  • CPET if high-risk or uncertainty remains
⚠ Escalate if: 6MWT <400 m; AT ≤11.1 mL/kg/min; active respiratory decompensation; uncontrolled heart failure or ischaemia
🎯 100% of esophagectomy patients must have explicit pulmonary risk review
⬡ 3.5 Oncologic Timing & Treatment Fitness ▶
  • Date of last chemotherapy / radiotherapy
  • Recovery from treatment toxicity
  • Interval weight loss during neoadjuvant
  • Interval decline in intake or function
✓ Timing targets: after neoadjuvant chemotherapy → surgery 3–6 weeks; after chemoradiotherapy / radiotherapy → surgery 6–10 weeks after last radiation
⛔ Do not move directly from restaging to theatre booking without a formal physiological reassessment
Step 4: Pathway assignment
✓ Ready for Surgery Now

All domains clear. No outstanding red flags. MDT confirmed. Labs within targets. Anaesthetic review signed off.

⟳ Fit After Optimisation

One or more modifiable issues present. Proceed to Step 5 optimisation package before final scheduling.

✗ Not Currently Fit

Major fitness concerns or non-modifiable risk. Re-discuss treatment strategy with MDT. Consider alternatives.

⚠ Any one of these triggers "optimisation first": ASA III–IV without consultant review · ECOG ≥2 · current smoking · oral intake <50% · severe dysphagia · Hb below target · 6MWT <400 m · AT ≤11.1 · uncontrolled comorbidity · clear frailty signal
Step 5: Optimisation package
5.1
Nutrition Optimisation
  • Oral counselling + oral nutritional supplements → first line if gut usable
  • Enteral support (NG/NJ/PEG/jej) if oral inadequate but gut functional
  • Parenteral nutrition if enteral not feasible or insufficient
⛔ Start nutritional therapy immediately if: nil orally expected ≥5 days OR intake <50% for >7 days
🎯 Recheck weight weekly during optimisation. Recheck intake at every contact.
5.2
Exercise & Respiratory Prehabilitation
  • Walking / aerobic conditioning programme
  • Inspiratory muscle training (IMT)
  • Breathing exercises, cough training
  • Secretion clearance techniques
  • Bronchodilator optimisation if indicated
⚠ Prioritise prehabilitation for: 6MWT <400 m; AT ≤11.1; severe malnutrition; ASA III/IV older adults
🎯 Every high-risk esophagectomy patient receives structured respiratory prehabilitation. Completion checked before surgery.
5.3
Smoking & Alcohol Cessation
  • Smoking cessation target: ≥4 weeks before surgery
  • Alcohol cessation target: ≥4 weeks before surgery
  • Active pharmacological + behavioural support offered
  • Quit date documented in all active smokers
🎯 Smoking status documented in 100% of patients. Current smokers receive active cessation support in 100%.
5.4
Anaemia & Medical Optimisation
  • Work up and treat iron deficiency, B12/folate deficiency
  • Treat symptomatic anaemia — target Hb ≥120 g/L both sexes
  • Optimise diabetes control (target HbA1c where feasible)
  • Optimise CKD/dehydration before major surgery
  • Stabilise COPD/asthma, unstable cardiac disease
⛔ Hb <120 g/L = anaemia work-up and correction plan. Unstable medical disease = defer final scheduling until optimised.
Step 6: Reassessment after neoadjuvant therapy or optimisation
Repeat at reassessment
  • Weight, BMI, intake
  • Dysphagia / obstructive symptoms
  • Hb, creatinine/eGFR, electrolytes
  • Albumin if relevant
  • ECOG, frailty / functional reserve
  • Respiratory fitness if initially impaired
  • Treatment toxicity resolved?
Final decision — document explicitly
✓Proceed — all targets met, fit for surgery
⟳Optimise further — one or more issues outstanding
✗Re-discuss treatment strategy — major fitness concern remains
🎯100% formal reassessment after neoadjuvant therapy. 100% formal reassessment after optimisation if surgery was delayed.
Phase B — Final 72–24 hours before surgery
Steps 7–9
Finalise Plan, Labs & Fasting
  • Step 7: Exact operation, approach, reconstruction plan, anastomotic site, postoperative destination, postoperative feeding plan — all documented
  • Step 8: Final labs — CBC, creatinine/eGFR, electrolytes, coagulation if indicated, G&X per local policy. Hb should meet target or have documented reason to proceed.
  • Step 9: Clear fluids until 2 h before anaesthesia; solids withheld 6 h before. Individualise for severe dysphagia / retained contents / GOO / delayed gastric emptying.
⛔No patient proceeds with missing core labs. Major renal/electrolyte abnormality requires active sign-off.
Steps 10–11
Optimisation Completion Check & Final Handover
  • Smoking cessation status confirmed
  • Alcohol cessation status confirmed
  • Nutrition plan started and documented
  • Anaemia plan completed or explicitly reviewed
  • Respiratory prehabilitation completed if indicated
  • Consultant anaesthetic review completed (ASA III–IV)
  • Consent updated to reflect final plan and risk discussion
Handover to Anaesthesia / ICU / Ward must state:
  • Pulmonary risk level
  • Nutritional risk level
  • Frailty signal
  • Aspiration risk
  • Need for ICU/HDU vs ward
  • Postoperative support plan
  • Any specific concern for anastomotic healing
🎯Standardised written handover in 100% of esophagectomies and all high-risk gastrectomies.

Phase D
Postoperative Monitoring & Care
Phase-based recovery algorithm with daily targets and escalation triggers
POD 0 — Immediate postoperative
Stabilise circulation, oxygenation, analgesia, drains
  • ICU / HDU / PACU monitoring, telemetry
  • Multimodal analgesia started (epidural / paravertebral active)
  • Drain check — output, appearance, gas noted
  • Lactate review — immediate and 6 h
  • Urine output review (target >0.5 mL/kg/h)
  • Head-of-bed elevation ≥30°
  • Respiratory exercises started
  • Sit out of bed ~2 h if feasible — evening of surgery
⚠MAP target ≥70 mmHg. Arterial-line monitoring through POD 2 when needed. Reassess immediately if hypotension, low urine output, or clinical deterioration.
POD 1–4 — Early recovery
Prevent pulmonary & cardiac complications; establish recovery momentum
  • Standard CBC + metabolic panel on POD 1 — aggressive electrolyte replacement
  • Continuous telemetry maintained
  • Respiratory therapy scheduled — incentive spirometry hourly while in bed
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation twice daily
  • Ambulation target: 4× per day from POD 1
  • Head-of-bed ≥30° maintained
  • Begin / advance feeding pathway (enteral preferred)
  • Daily drain assessment — output, colour, amylase if suspected leak
  • Consider NG tube removal when feasible
POD 5–7 — Transition phase
Transition from acute care to functional recovery
  • Convert toward enteral / oral-compatible analgesia (via jejunostomy if present)
  • Continue opioid-sparing principles
  • Mobilisation target: 6× per day
  • Spend up to 50% of day out of bed
  • Nutritional route clearly defined and documented
  • Review drains and tubes — remove when safe (avoid prolonged chest drain)
  • Contrast swallow considered if oral pathway planned (if not clear on earlier POD)
  • No unresolved haemodynamic instability
POD ≥8 — Discharge readiness
Confirm functional recovery, not calendar target
  • Drain plan finalised and documented
  • Feeding plan confirmed (oral / enteral at home)
  • Patient education completed (jejunostomy care, feeding regimen)
  • Home support / facility arranged
  • Sit out of bed up to 80% of day before discharge
  • Stable on ward-level care only
  • Clear follow-up and escalation instructions given
  • No untreated septic or respiratory deterioration
💡Discharge readiness = mobility + respiratory stability + feeding tolerance + drain plan + clear follow-up. Not a fixed postoperative day.

●Oliguria unexplained

When escalation trigger identified
Step 1
Clinical review by responsible surgeon — document assessment
Step 2
Emergency labs: CBC, CRP, procalcitonin, lactate, drain amylase, bilirubin
Step 3
Drain review — output volume, colour, amylase, gas — document explicitly
Step 4
CT with IV ± oral contrast and/or endoscopy when indicated — do not delay
Step 5
ICU upgrade if needed — do not wait for clinical deterioration to become critical
Step 6
Early source-control planning — do not adopt serial passive observation

Anastomotic Leak & Conduit Necrosis — Detailed Instrument
Comprehensive data capture for anastomotic failure sub-study with treatment modality analysis
ECCG Anastomotic Leak Classification
Full-thickness GI defect involving oesophagus, anastomosis, staple line, or conduit — irrespective of presentation or method of identification
IType I: Local defect — no change in therapy; treated medically or with dietary modification only
IIType II: Localised defect — interventional but not surgical therapy (IR drain, stent, bedside opening and packing of incision)
IIIType III: Localised defect — requires surgical therapy
ECCG Conduit Necrosis Classification
IType I: Focal necrosis identified endoscopically — additional monitoring or non-surgical therapy
IIType II: Focal necrosis identified endoscopically, not associated with free leak — surgical therapy, no oesophageal diversion
IIIType III: Extensive conduit necrosis — conduit resection with diversion (cervical oesophagostomy)

Sub-study analysis framework
  • Primary endpoint: ECCG leak type at first confirmed event
  • Treatment analysis: Compare conservative vs endoscopic (stent vs EVT) vs surgical management — by leak type and conduit status
  • Timing analysis: POD of suspicion → POD confirmed → POD source control
  • Quality metric: Time (hours) from suspicion to first source control — stratify by detection method
  • Conduit perfusion correlation: Link intraop ICG findings / surgeon concern to leak occurrence
  • Anastomotic technique comparison: Hand-sewn vs circular vs linear stapled — leak rate, ECCG type, time to resolution
  • Failure to rescue capture: Link all leak episodes to overall 30/90-day mortality outcome
📌Every patient with a confirmed leak or conduit necrosis episode must have the full leak instrument completed, not just the complication flag in the main database
